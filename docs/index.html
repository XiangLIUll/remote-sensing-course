Remote Sensing for Plant Diversity Prediction (R)

Hands-on module · Part of the Advanced Remote Sensing course

Overview

This hands-on course introduces students to remote sensing workflows for predicting plant diversity in R. We will connect field-based plant diversity metrics with multispectral satellite data, build predictive models, and communicate results using reproducible R scripts.

This topic is designed as the final chapter of the Advanced Remote Sensing course .

R Remote sensing Plant diversity Machine learning Geospatial analysis

Audience & Prerequisites

Audience

  • Bachelor students with interest in ecology, geography, or geoinformatics.

Prerequisites

  • Working knowledge of R (data wrangling, basic plotting).
  • Basic GIS concepts and coordinate reference systems (CRS).
  • R (≥ 4.2) with packages: terra, sf, tidyverse, caret (or tidymodels), rasterdiv.

Learning Outcomes

  • [Compute plant diversity metrics (richness, Shannon, Simpson, Hill numbers) in R](https://github.com/XiangLIUll/remote-sensing-course/blob/main/R%20code/week1_lab.html).
  • Acquire and preprocess multispectral satellite imagery for vegetation studies.
  • Derive spectral indices (NDVI, EVI), spectral diversity, and texture metrics.
  • Build species richness models by linking field plots and raster predictors.
  • Evaluate models with cross-validation and spatial blocking.
  • Produce prediction maps, quantify uncertainty, and communicate results.

Schedule (3 weeks · 2 hours per week)

Week 1 – Species Diversity & Preprocessing

  • Course orientation and data overview.
  • Calculating species diversity metrics in R.
  • Computing spectral indices (NDVI, EVI) and basic raster statistics.
  • Lab: Reproducible preprocessing script for the study area.

Week 2 – Feature Engineering & Model Design

  • Linking plot-based diversity data with raster predictors using sf.
  • Creating texture and landscape metrics.
  • Exploratory data analysis and collinearity checks.
  • Modelling species richness with caret / tidymodels.
  • Lab: Baseline models and (spatial) cross-validation.

Week 3 – Assessment, Mapping & Communication

  • Model diagnostics: variable importance, partial dependence.
  • Uncertainty and spatial residual analysis.
  • Generating and exporting prediction maps.
  • Capstone: End-to-end workflow with provided datasets.
